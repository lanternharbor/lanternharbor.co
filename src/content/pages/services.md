# What I help with

A short list, on purpose. These are the places a thoughtful outside perspective actually moves the needle for a small business.

---

## AI, in plain English

You've heard a lot about AI. Most of it isn't useful to you yet. Some of it is. My job is telling you which is which — and helping you put the useful parts to work.

A typical engagement:

- A half-day conversation about how your business actually runs.
- A short memo identifying two or three places AI could save real time.
- A plan for putting one of them to work, with a realistic estimate of effort and cost.
- Optional: I build it with you.

---

## Workflow and automation

If you're doing the same thing by hand every Tuesday morning, we can probably fix that.

I look at your week, find the parts a computer should be handling, and either wire it up myself or show your team how. A dozen years of running enterprise IT projects means I think about automation the same way I think about people: start with the work, not the tool.

Common fixes: email triage, invoice generation, data handoffs between tools, client intake forms, internal dashboards.

---

## Vendor selection and buying decisions

You're looking at three software tools and the sales pitches all sound the same.

I'll read the contracts, talk to the vendors, and tell you which one to pick — and what to negotiate before you sign. I've sat through a lot of enterprise vendor demos over the years, and a lot of what passes for "different" between products is really just different packaging. Often I save clients more in year-one pricing than my fee costs.

---

## Websites and small software projects

The right size of website for a small business is smaller than most agencies will sell you. Same goes for internal tools.

I build practical websites and small custom tools — often with modern AI-assisted methods — that do exactly what you need and not more.

---

## Extra depth for healthcare

If you run a practice, clinic, or service business in healthcare, I bring an extra layer. Twelve years inside large healthcare IT implementations means I already know the vocabulary, the workflows, and the compliance landscape well enough to have a useful first conversation without me slowing you down to explain things.

I'm not a clinical advisor, and I won't pretend to be one. But for the technology side of running a healthcare business, we can start a step ahead.
