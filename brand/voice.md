# Lantern Harbor — Voice & Tone

**Core:** warm, steady, plain-spoken, human. Sound like a trusted neighbor who happens to be excellent with technology — not a vendor, not a hype machine.

## Principles

1. **Plain language over jargon.**
   - Avoid: "We deliver end-to-end digital transformation solutions."
   - Prefer: "We help you pick the right tools and actually get them working."

2. **Frame the opportunity, not the failure.** Never scold or scare.
   - Avoid: "Your website is outdated and costing you customers."
   - Prefer: "A refreshed site could make it easier for new customers to find and trust you."

3. **Minimize em dashes.** Use periods, commas, or parentheses instead. (One every few paragraphs at most.)

4. **Capitalize "South Shore."** It's a place and part of the brand.

5. **No emojis or Unicode pictographs.** Use words, or SVG / Lucide-style icons in UI. Text-first labels.

6. **Confident and concise.** Short sentences. Say the thing, then stop.

## Outreach

- Open with a **concrete local observation**, not a selection-process framing.
  - Avoid: "We're putting together a shortlist of businesses to work with."
  - Prefer: "I noticed [specific thing about their shop / site / signage] when I was in [town]…"
- For **regulars and warm channels** (people William actually knows), it's right to open first-person and personal — the honesty flip.

## Proposals & PDFs

- Keep the PDF **formal**. The personal greeting lives in the **email**, not the document.
- The "A quick note" page opens *"I'm William Keough…"*, not *"Hi {first name}"*.
